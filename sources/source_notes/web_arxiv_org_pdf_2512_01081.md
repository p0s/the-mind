## One-paragraph summary
Research-program paper that proposes a computational-functional framing of machine consciousness and a long-term validation approach. It treats the Machine Consciousness Hypothesis as a substrate-independent functional claim (consciousness as second-order perception) and motivates “in silico” investigation via simulated environments and distributed learning systems. A central through-line is that self-models and conscious-like organization should be studied as emergent phenomena in communication and coordination among predictive subsystems, rather than as a property of an isolated model. The paper situates this program in a broader computationalist tradition and frames testability as an empirical project: design environments, build candidate architectures, specify observable signatures, and iteratively refine hypotheses about what organizational features are necessary.
