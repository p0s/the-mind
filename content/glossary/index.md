# Glossary

This glossary is intentionally short.
It defines the terms that carry the most weight in V2.

## Agency
We will use **agency** to mean: the capacity of a system to regulate the future using models, rather than only reacting to the present. <!-- src: ccc_38c3_self_models_of_loving_grace @ 00:32:16 -->

## Attention
We will use **attention** to mean: the selective allocation of limited processing resources so that some contents become more available for control, learning, and coordination. <!-- src: web_cimc_ai_cimchypothesis_pdf @ p16-18 -->

## Coherence
We will use **coherence** to mean: a reduction of contradiction or constraint violation among simultaneously active mental contents, so the system can act more like one agent. <!-- src: web_cimc_ai_cimchypothesis_pdf @ p16-18 -->

## Computationalism
We will use **computationalism** to mean: the view that the relevant organization of mind can, in principle, be implemented as computation rather than being tied only to one biological material. <!-- src: web_cimc_ai_cimchypothesis_pdf @ p6-8 -->

## Computationalist functionalism
We will use **computationalist functionalism** to mean: the joint stance that conscious mind depends on functional organization, and that such organization may be implementable on computational machines. This does not imply that current computers are conscious. <!-- src: web_cimc_ai_cimchypothesis_pdf @ p6-8 -->

## Consciousness
We will use **consciousness** to mean: a special mode of mental organization in which content is present to the system as present, often via second-order perception, and in which mental states are made more coherent for agency. <!-- src: web_cimc_ai_cimchypothesis_pdf @ p15-18; web_cimc_ai_cimchypothesis_pdf @ p20 -->

## Feeling
We will use **feeling** to mean: a consciously accessible evaluation of reality and the self that carries valence and shapes attention, learning, and action. <!-- src: web_cimc_ai_cimchypothesis_pdf @ p13 -->

## Free will
We will use **free will** to mean: the capacity of an agent to act according to higher-order commitments rather than being overruled by compulsive local loops. <!-- src: ccc_35c3_10030_the_ghost_in_the_machine @ 00:36:40 -->

## Genesis Hypothesis
We will use **Genesis Hypothesis** to mean: the idea that consciousness appears early in development and may play an instrumental role in building coherent reality-models, selfhood, and intelligent agency. <!-- src: web_cimc_ai_cimchypothesis_pdf @ p18-20 -->

## Imagination
We will use **imagination** to mean: hypothetical or internally generated world-content produced by the same broad modeling machinery as perception, but with weaker present sensory constraint. <!-- src: web_cimc_ai_cimchypothesis_pdf @ p13-15 -->

## Intellect
We will use **intellect** to mean: the more explicit, reflective, concept-using aspect of the mind associated with symbolic thought, planning, and analysis. It is part of mind, not the whole of it. <!-- src: web_cimc_ai_cimchypothesis_pdf @ p12-13 -->

## Mind
We will use **mind** to mean: the larger modeling-and-control matrix in which perceptions, feelings, thoughts, intuitions, imaginations, self-models, and action policies are organized for agency. <!-- src: web_cimc_ai_cimchypothesis_pdf @ p12-15; ccc_38c3_self_models_of_loving_grace @ 00:32:16 -->

## Perception
We will use **perception** to mean: the structuring of sensory input into usable representation. In this framework, perception is already constructive rather than a raw copy of physics. <!-- src: web_cimc_ai_cimchypothesis_pdf @ p15 -->

## Phenomenal reality
We will use **phenomenal reality** to mean: sensory representation that is currently being confirmed. <!-- src: web_cimc_ai_cimchypothesis_pdf @ p15 -->

## Presence / presentness
We will use **presence** or **presentness** to mean: the felt fact that experience is happening now; one of the central phenomenological markers of consciousness. <!-- src: web_cimc_ai_cimchypothesis_pdf @ p15; web_cimc_ai_cimchypothesis_pdf @ p20 -->

## Psyche
We will use **psyche** to mean: the combination of a personal self and the motivational “strings” that pull on it, within a mind that models self, interests, and world. <!-- src: web_cimc_ai_cimchypothesis_pdf @ p13 -->

## Qualia
We will use **qualia** to mean: the qualitative character of experience, what a state is like from the inside. <!-- src: yt_CcQMYNi9a2w @ 01:58:03 -->

## Realness
We will use **realness** to mean: the representation of something currently being the case. <!-- src: web_cimc_ai_cimchypothesis_pdf @ p15 -->

## Self
We will use **self** to mean: the model of the agent as me — with a point of view, boundaries, concerns, commitments, and a story across time. <!-- src: yt_UkAOHrbcnAs @ 00:16:47; yt_CcQMYNi9a2w @ 00:25:57 -->

## Self-model
We will use **self-model** to mean: the explicit or implicit representation a mind builds of itself as an agent inside its own world-model. <!-- src: yt_UkAOHrbcnAs @ 00:16:47 -->

## Second-order perception
We will use **second-order perception** to mean: awareness not only of content, but of the fact that representation is taking place now. <!-- src: web_cimc_ai_cimchypothesis_pdf @ p14-15; web_cimc_ai_cimchypothesis_pdf @ p20 -->

## Third-order perception
We will use **third-order perception** to mean: awareness of the self as the observer within the act of observation — a more explicit first-person standpoint. <!-- src: web_cimc_ai_cimchypothesis_pdf @ p15-16 -->

## Valence
We will use **valence** to mean: the positive or negative significance of states for the system; the broad way a mind marks what counts as better, worse, urgent, dangerous, or rewarding. <!-- src: web_cimc_ai_cimchypothesis_pdf @ p13; ccc_35c3_10030_the_ghost_in_the_machine @ 00:27:50 -->

## Virtualism
We will use **virtualism** to mean: the view that the experienced world and self are real as implemented model-objects or causal structure, not as separate substances outside physics. <!-- src: ccc_37c3_12167_synthetic_sentience @ 00:17:39; yt_b6oekXIQ-LM @ 00:19:53 -->
